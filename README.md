Technology follows the philosophy of the civilization using it.

This paper presents dualism as the destructive trajectory of modern science and proposes **Wa**, together with the Six Principles—Natural Law, Harmony, Circulation, Structure, Order, and Wa—as an alternative civilizational foundation.

## 2. How Science Became an Instrument of Domination

### 2.1 Nuclear Science

The discovery of nuclear fission was neutral as an understanding of natural law. However, its first large-scale application was the atomic bomb.

“Peaceful use” appeared later as a form of moral repair. This was not caused by science itself, but by civilization’s priorities.

### 2.2 Chemistry

The true purpose of chemistry is to understand matter and its interactions.

However, under dualistic civilization, chemistry was directed toward poison gas, biological agents, herbicidal warfare, and persistent pollutants.

Control, efficiency, and military advantage were prioritized over natural circulation and microbial harmony.

### 2.3 Aviation

Humanity’s aspiration to fly originally expanded the possibilities of mobility, rescue, exchange, and observation.

Yet aviation quickly moved toward bombers, fighters, and military drones.

Flight became not only a technology of connection, but also a technology of attack.

### 2.4 Artificial Intelligence

The true potential of AI is to complement human intelligence and support environmental observation, consensus-building, education, medicine, disaster response, and civilizational design.

However, under dualistic competitive structures, AI is easily directed toward surveillance, autonomous weapons, psychological manipulation, information control, and labor exploitation.

AI itself is not the evil.  
The problem is operating AI under a civilizational OS of domination.

## 5. Edo Japan: Evidence of Non-Dualistic Science

Edo-period Japan was not a perfect society. However, it is important as a historical reference point for non-dualistic scientific civilization.

### Wasan

Wasan, or Japanese mathematics, was highly advanced, yet it was not centralized around artillery, ballistics, or military domination. It was applied to commerce, architecture, surveying, agriculture, and engineering.

### Medicine

Medicine was primarily directed toward healing and recovery. The ideological pressure toward biological weapons or unethical experimentation was much weaker than in modern militarized science.

### Astronomy

Astronomy was used for calendar accuracy, agriculture, and seasonal understanding. It functioned more as a tool for life harmonization than conquest or religious domination.

### Metallurgy

Advanced metalworking was directed toward tools, craft, beauty, and daily life rather than mass weaponry as the central path.

These examples show that when the underlying philosophy is harmony, science can evolve as a constructive force.
