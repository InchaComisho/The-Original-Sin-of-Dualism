Abstract

This paper examines the foundational flaw embedded in Western civilization: dualism—the belief that the world is divided into opposing binaries such as good vs. evil, us vs. them, domination vs. submission.

Master identifies dualism not as a philosophical error, but as a systemic bias that shaped the direction of science, technology, politics, and cultural evolution for thousands of years.

Dualism transformed science into an instrument of domination.
By contrast, Wa—the philosophy of harmony, circulation, and coexistence—would have produced a completely different scientific civilization.

The central thesis:

Technology is neutral.
The direction of civilization is determined entirely by the underlying philosophy.

Thus, the future of humanity depends not on new inventions, but on the transformation of its foundational worldview.

1. Dualism: The Structural Distortion of Human Civilization

Western civilization was built on binary thinking:
	•	good vs. evil
	•	winners vs. losers
	•	humans vs. nature
	•	civilization vs. wilderness
	•	progress vs. stagnation

This cognitive template shaped scientific progress for centuries.

Dualism → Science of domination

Examples illustrate the pattern clearly:

Nuclear science
	•	Neutral discovery: nuclear fission
	•	First application: atomic bombs
	•	“Peaceful use” came later as moral repair

Chemistry
	•	True purpose: understanding matter
	•	Early applications: poison gas, biological agents, herbicidal warfare

Aviation
	•	Origin: human aspiration to fly
	•	Early applications: bombers, fighters, military drones

Artificial Intelligence
	•	True potential: augmentation of human intelligence
	•	Early applications: surveillance, autonomous weapons, large-scale psychological manipulation

This is not a coincidence.
Technology follows ideology.
Dualism ensured that scientific discoveries were directed toward domination, not harmony.

Medicine
